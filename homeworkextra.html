Hindi (Devanagari: हिन्दी, हिंदी, ISO: Hindī), or more precisely Modern Standard Hindi (Devanagari: मानक हिन्दी, ISO: Mānak Hindī),[8] is an Indo-Aryan language spoken chiefly in the North and Central India. Hindi has been described as a standardised and Sanskritised register[9] of the Hindustani language, which itself is based primarily on the Khariboli

dialect of Delhi and neighbouring areas of Northern India.[10][11][12] Hindi, written in the Devanagari script, is one of the two official languages of the Government of India, along with English.[13] It is an official language in 9 States and 3 Union Territories and an additional official language in 3 other States.[14][15][16][17] Hindi is also one of the 22 scheduled languages of the Republic of India.[18]

Hindi is the lingua franca of the Hindi Belt.
